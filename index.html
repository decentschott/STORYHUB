Submission Tips

What separates a great story
from a good idea

Concrete details, real people, and measurable impact are what make a story stand out and get published.

Do This
  • Be specific — include numbers, outcomes, and concrete details
  • Show impact — explain who benefits and how
  • Think about external audiences: media, donors, prospective students
  • Put people at the center — stories about people resonate more than programs
  • Provide context — even if it seems obvious to you, spell it out
  • Submit early — give the team time to produce quality content
Avoid This
  • Being too general — "we are doing great work" is not a story
  • Using jargon — write as if you are explaining to an outsider
  • Overselling — let the facts speak for themselves
  • Submitting right before a deadline — last-minute stories are harder to tell
  • Ignoring follow-up requests for more information
  • Assuming the team already knows the background

Strong submission example: "Dr. Maria Rodriguez's team developed a free app helping bilingual families access educational resources. In six months: 15,000 downloads, adopted by school districts statewide."
